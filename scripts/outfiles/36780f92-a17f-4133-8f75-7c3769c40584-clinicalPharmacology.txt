
               
               
               
                  12 CLINICAL PHARMACOLOGY                    12.1 Mechanism of Action          The mechanism of action of meloxicam, like that of other NSAIDs, may be related to prostaglandin synthetase (cyclo-oxygenase) inhibition
which is involved in the initial steps of the arachidonic acid cascade,
resulting in the reduced formation of prostaglandins, thromboxanes and
prostacylin. It is not completely understood how reduced synthesis of
these compounds results in therapeutic efficacy.
