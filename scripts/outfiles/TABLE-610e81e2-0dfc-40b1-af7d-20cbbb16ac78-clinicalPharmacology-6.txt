<sup>§§</sup> Staphylococci which are resistant to methicillin/oxacillin must be considered as resistant to amoxicillin/clavulanic acid.<br/>
<sup>a</sup> A broth microdilution method should be used for testing <content stylecode="italics">H. influenzae</content>. Beta-lactamase- negative, ampicillin-resistant strains must be considered resistant to amoxicillin/clavulanic acid.<br/>
<sup>b</sup> Susceptibility of <content stylecode="italics">S. pneumoniae</content> should be determined using a 1 mcg oxacillin disk. Isolates with oxacillin zone sizes of ≥20 mm are susceptible to amoxicillin/clavulanic acid. An amoxicillin/clavulanic acid MIC should be determined on isolates of <content stylecode="italics">S. pneumoniae</content> with oxacillin zone sizes of ≤19 mm.<br/>
<sup>c</sup> A broth microdilution method should be used for testing <content stylecode="italics">N. gonorrhoeae</content> and interpreted according to penicillin breakpoints.</td>
