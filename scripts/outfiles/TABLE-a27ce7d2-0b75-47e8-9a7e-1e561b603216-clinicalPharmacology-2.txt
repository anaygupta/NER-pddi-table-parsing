<td align="left" colspan="7" stylecode="Botrule" valign="top">S = Susceptible, I = Intermediate, R = Resistant<br/>No interpretative criteria have been established for testing enterococci.<br/>Susceptibility of staphylococci to meropenem may be deduced from testing penicillin and either cefoxitin or oxacillin.<br/>
<sup>a</sup> The interpretive criteria for <content stylecode="italics">P. aeruginosa </content>are based upon the dosing of 1 gram every 8 hours.<br/>
<sup>b</sup> The current absence of data on resistant isolates precludes defining any category other than “Susceptible”. If isolates yield MIC results other than susceptible, they should be submitted to a reference laboratory for additional testing.<br/>
<sup>c</sup> For nonmeningitis isolates of <content stylecode="italics">S. pneumoniae</content> a penicillin MIC of ≤ 0.06 mcg/mL or oxacillin zone ≥ 20 mm can predict susceptibility to meropenem. MIC testing should be performed on isolates that do not test as susceptible by either of these methods, and on all meningitis <content stylecode="italics">S. pneumoniae</content> isolates.<br/>
<sup>d</sup> Viridans group streptococci should be tested for meropenem susceptibility using a MIC method and results should be reported using the interpretive criteria listed for <content stylecode="italics">S. agalactiae </content>and <content stylecode="italics">S. pyogenes</content>.<br/>
<sup>e</sup> Reliable disk diffusion tests for meropenem do not yet exist for testing streptococci.<br/>
<sup>f</sup> MIC values using either Brucella blood or Wilkins Chalgren agar (former reference medium) are considered equivalent, based upon published <content stylecode="italics">in vitro</content> literature and a multicenter collaborative trial for these antimicrobial agents. Broth microdilution is only recommended for testing the <content stylecode="italics">B. fragilis </content>group. MIC values for agar or broth microdilution are considered equivalent for that group.</td>
