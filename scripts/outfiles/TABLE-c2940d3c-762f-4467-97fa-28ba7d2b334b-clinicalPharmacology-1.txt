<paragraph>*   For oxacillin-susceptible <content stylecode="italics">
<content stylecode="emphasis">S. aureus </content>
</content>and coagulase negative staphylococci results for carbapenems, including imipenem, if tested, should be reported according to the results generated using routine interpretive criteria. For oxacillin-resistant <content stylecode="italics">
<content stylecode="emphasis">S. aureus </content>
</content>and coagulase negative staphylococci, other beta lactam agents, including carbapenems, may appear active <content stylecode="italics">
<content stylecode="emphasis">in vitro </content>
</content>but are not effective clinically. Results for beta lactam agents other than cephalosporins with anti-MRSA activity should be reported as resistant or should not be reported. </paragraph>
<paragraph>
<sup>†     </sup>For some organism/antimicrobial combinations, the absence or rare occurrence of resistant strains precludes defining any results categories other than “susceptible”. For strains yielding results suggestive of a “non-susceptible” category, organism identification and antimicrobial susceptibility test results should be confirmed. </paragraph>

<content stylecode="emphasis">S. pneumoniae </content>
